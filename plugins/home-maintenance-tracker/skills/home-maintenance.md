# Home Maintenance Skill

Comprehensive patterns and best practices for home maintenance scheduling, warranty tracking, and service history management.

## Purpose
Provide expert knowledge for managing all aspects of home maintenance including schedules, warranties, service records, and contractor relationships.

---

## 1. Maintenance Schedules by Home System

### HVAC System
**Monthly**:
- Replace/clean air filters (or per manufacturer spec)
- Check thermostat operation
- Verify proper airflow from vents

**Quarterly**:
- Clean around outdoor condenser unit
- Check condensate drain line
- Test heating/cooling cycle

**Bi-Annual** (Spring & Fall):
- Professional HVAC service
- Clean coils and blower components
- Check refrigerant levels
- Test safety controls
- Lubricate moving parts

**Annual**:
- Duct inspection and cleaning (if needed)
- Seal any duct leaks
- Check insulation on refrigerant lines

### Plumbing System
**Monthly**:
- Check for leaks under sinks
- Test all faucets and fixtures
- Inspect exposed pipes for corrosion

**Quarterly**:
- Flush drain lines with hot water/cleaner
- Check water pressure
- Inspect toilet tank components

**Bi-Annual**:
- Test sump pump (if applicable)
- Check washing machine hoses
- Inspect water heater for leaks/corrosion

**Annual**:
- Flush water heater tank
- Test pressure relief valve
- Professional septic inspection (if applicable)
- Check main water shut-off valve

### Electrical System
**Monthly**:
- Test GFCI outlets
- Check for flickering lights or issues

**Quarterly**:
- Inspect visible wiring for damage
- Test arc-fault circuit breakers

**Annual**:
- Test whole-house surge protector
- Professional electrical inspection (every 3-5 years)
- Check outdoor outlet covers

### Roofing & Gutters
**Quarterly**:
- Visual roof inspection from ground
- Clear debris from roof valleys

**Bi-Annual** (Spring & Fall):
- Clean gutters and downspouts
- Check gutter attachment and slope
- Inspect roof for damaged/missing shingles
- Clear debris from roof

**Annual**:
- Professional roof inspection
- Check flashing around vents and chimney
- Inspect attic for leaks or moisture

### Lawn & Landscaping
**Weekly** (Growing Season):
- Mow lawn (maintain 3-4 inch height)
- Edge walkways and beds
- Water as needed (1-1.5 inches per week)

**Monthly**:
- Fertilize (per seasonal schedule)
- Weed control
- Trim shrubs and hedges

**Quarterly**:
- Mulch beds
- Prune trees and large shrubs
- Aerate lawn (spring and fall)

**Bi-Annual**:
- Overseed lawn (spring or fall)
- Clean out flower beds
- Apply pre-emergent herbicide

### Appliances

**Refrigerator**:
- Monthly: Clean interior, check door seals
- Quarterly: Clean condenser coils, empty drip pan
- Bi-annual: Check temperature accuracy (37-40°F fridge, 0°F freezer)

**Dishwasher**:
- Monthly: Clean filter and spray arms
- Quarterly: Run cleaning cycle with dishwasher cleaner
- Annual: Check door seals and hoses

**Washing Machine**:
- Monthly: Run cleaning cycle, wipe door seal
- Quarterly: Check hoses for bulges or leaks
- Annual: Clean drain pump filter

**Dryer**:
- After every load: Clean lint trap
- Monthly: Vacuum lint from around drum
- Quarterly: Clean vent duct and exterior vent
- Annual: Professional vent cleaning

## 2. Seasonal Maintenance Checklists

### Spring (March - May)

**Exterior**:
- Clean gutters and downspouts
- Inspect roof for winter damage
- Check siding and paint for damage
- Power wash deck, patio, siding
- Service lawn mower and lawn equipment
- Check sprinkler system, repair leaks
- Inspect foundation for cracks
- Reseal driveway and walkways if needed

**Interior**:
- Replace HVAC filters
- Schedule HVAC cooling system service
- Test smoke and CO detectors
- Clean ceiling fans (switch to summer rotation)
- Deep clean windows (inside and out)
- Check attic ventilation
- Service garage door opener

**Lawn & Garden**:
- Aerate and overseed lawn
- Apply pre-emergent herbicide
- Start fertilization schedule
- Prune trees and shrubs
- Mulch garden beds
- Plant spring flowers and vegetables

### Summer (June - August)

**Exterior**:
- Monitor and adjust irrigation
- Maintain lawn weekly
- Clean outdoor furniture
- Check deck/patio for needed repairs
- Paint or stain as needed
- Inspect exterior lighting

**Interior**:
- Replace HVAC filters monthly
- Service dehumidifier
- Deep clean refrigerator coils
- Check and reseal windows/doors
- Clean dryer vent

**Lawn & Garden**:
- Maintain 3-4 inch grass height
- Water deeply, less frequently
- Continue weed control
- Deadhead flowers
- Harvest vegetables
- Monitor for pests and diseases

### Fall (September - November)

**Exterior**:
- Clean gutters after leaves fall
- Inspect and repair roof before winter
- Winterize sprinkler system
- Store or cover outdoor furniture
- Seal cracks in driveway and walkway
- Check chimney and fireplace
- Trim tree branches away from house

**Interior**:
- Schedule HVAC heating system service
- Replace HVAC filters
- Reverse ceiling fan rotation
- Check weatherstripping on doors/windows
- Test and replace smoke/CO detector batteries
- Inspect and service fireplace/chimney
- Flush water heater

**Lawn & Garden**:
- Final mowing (lower blade slightly)
- Aerate and overseed lawn
- Apply fall fertilizer
- Rake and remove leaves
- Plant spring bulbs
- Winterize garden beds
- Store garden tools

### Winter (December - February)

**Exterior**:
- Clear ice and snow from walkways
- Remove snow from roof if excessive
- Monitor for ice dams
- Check for drafts around doors/windows
- Ensure proper ventilation in attic

**Interior**:
- Replace HVAC filters
- Maintain humidity levels (30-50%)
- Check for ice dams and address promptly
- Prevent frozen pipes (insulate, leave cabinet doors open in cold)
- Service humidifier
- Deep clean carpets and upholstery

**Planning**:
- Review service logs from previous year
- Plan spring projects
- Research contractors for upcoming work
- Update home inventory
- Review insurance coverage

## 3. Warranty Tracking Best Practices

### Documentation Requirements
- Purchase receipt with date
- Warranty certificate or documentation
- Serial number and model number
- Proof of registration (if required)
- Installer information (for workmanship warranties)

### Warranty Categories

**Appliance Warranties**:
- Standard: 1 year parts and labor
- Major components: 5-10 years (compressor, motor, etc.)
- Extended: Optional purchase, typically 2-5 years

**HVAC Warranties**:
- Standard: 5-10 years parts
- Labor: 1 year (often)
- Extended: Available through manufacturer or installer
- Registration requirement: Often must register within 90 days

**Roofing Warranties**:
- Materials: 20-50 years (prorated)
- Workmanship: 1-10 years (installer)
- Requirements: Regular maintenance, professional installation

**Water Heater Warranties**:
- Standard: 6-12 years
- Requirements: Annual maintenance may be required
- Prorated: Often prorated after certain period

**Windows/Doors**:
- Glass: Often lifetime limited
- Frame/hardware: 10-20 years typical
- Installation: 1-5 years

### Warranty Alert Strategy
- **90 days out**: Initial notification, start monitoring
- **60 days out**: Test all functions, document any issues
- **30 days out**: Schedule inspection if needed
- **7 days out**: Final reminder, file claims immediately

### Maximizing Warranty Value
1. Register all warranties promptly
2. Keep all documentation organized and accessible
3. Perform required maintenance
4. Test all functions before expiration
5. Document issues immediately
6. File claims well before expiration
7. Consider extended warranties for high-cost items
8. Use warranty-approved service providers

## 4. Service History Documentation

### Essential Service Log Elements
1. **Date and time** of service
2. **System/area** affected
3. **Service provider** details
4. **Work performed** (detailed description)
5. **Parts used** with costs
6. **Labor cost** and total cost
7. **Warranty on work** performed
8. **Next service recommendation**
9. **Photos** (before/after)
10. **Supporting documents** (invoice, receipt)

### Categorization System
**By System**:
- HVAC
- Plumbing
- Electrical
- Roofing
- Appliances
- Lawn/Landscape
- Structural
- Other

**By Type**:
- Preventive maintenance
- Repair
- Emergency repair
- Installation
- Upgrade/improvement
- Inspection

**By Provider**:
- Professional service
- DIY
- Under warranty

### Service History Benefits
1. **Maintenance planning**: Identify patterns and optimal schedules
2. **Resale value**: Documented maintenance increases home value
3. **Warranty claims**: Proof of maintenance for warranty coverage
4. **Budget forecasting**: Historical costs inform future budgets
5. **Contractor evaluation**: Track performance across multiple jobs
6. **System lifecycle**: Plan for replacements based on age and service history

## 5. Contractor Evaluation Criteria

### Pre-Hiring Evaluation
- [ ] Licensed for work in your area
- [ ] Insured (liability and workers comp)
- [ ] Bonded (if applicable)
- [ ] References from recent similar jobs
- [ ] Online reviews and ratings
- [ ] Better Business Bureau rating
- [ ] Written estimate with detailed scope
- [ ] Clear timeline and milestones
- [ ] Warranty on work performed

### During Service Evaluation
- Arrives on time
- Professional appearance and conduct
- Respects property (shoe covers, drop cloths)
- Explains work and answers questions
- Keeps work area clean
- Uses quality materials
- Follows safety protocols

### Post-Service Evaluation
- Work quality meets standards
- Completed within quoted time
- Final cost matches estimate
- Provides detailed invoice
- Cleans up thoroughly
- Offers warranty on work
- Responsive to any callbacks
- Would hire again

### Rating System
**5 Stars**: Exceptional - highest recommendation
**4 Stars**: Good - reliable and professional
**3 Stars**: Adequate - gets the job done
**2 Stars**: Below expectations - would not rehire
**1 Star**: Poor - significant issues

### Red Flags
- Requests large upfront payment
- No written contract or estimate
- Unlicensed or uninsured
- Pressure tactics
- Poor communication
- No references available
- Cash-only payment
- No physical business address

## 7. Cost Tracking and Budgeting

### Annual Maintenance Budget Rule
**1% Rule**: Budget 1% of home value annually for maintenance
**Example**: $400,000 home = $4,000/year maintenance budget

**Square Footage Rule**: $1-2 per square foot annually
**Example**: 2,000 sq ft home = $2,000-4,000/year

### Budget Categories
1. **Preventive Maintenance** (40%): Regular scheduled maintenance
2. **Repairs** (35%): Unplanned repairs and fixes
3. **Improvements** (15%): Upgrades and enhancements
4. **Emergency Fund** (10%): Unexpected major issues

### Age-Based Replacement Planning
- **HVAC**: 15-20 years ($5,000-10,000)
- **Water Heater**: 10-15 years ($800-2,000)
- **Roof**: 20-30 years ($8,000-20,000)
- **Appliances**: 10-15 years ($500-3,000 each)
- **Windows**: 20-25 years ($300-1,000 per window)
- **Exterior Paint**: 5-10 years ($3,000-8,000)

### Cost-Saving Strategies
1. Perform preventive maintenance to extend system life
2. DIY appropriate tasks (filter changes, basic cleaning)
3. Buy quality parts/materials for durability
4. Schedule non-emergency work during off-peak seasons
5. Bundle related tasks with same contractor
6. Maintain relationships with reliable contractors
7. Learn basic troubleshooting to avoid service calls
8. Keep systems clean and well-maintained

## 8. Emergency Preparedness

### Emergency Contacts
- Main water shutoff location
- Main electrical panel location
- Gas shutoff location (if applicable)
- Emergency plumber (24/7)
- Emergency electrician (24/7)
- Emergency HVAC service
- General contractor
- Insurance agent
- Alarm company

### Emergency Procedures

**Water Leak**:
1. Shut off main water valve
2. Turn off water heater
3. Call emergency plumber
4. Document damage (photos)
5. Notify insurance if major

**Electrical Issue**:
1. Turn off affected circuit
2. Unplug devices on circuit
3. Call licensed electrician
4. Never touch electrical hazards
5. Use flashlights, not candles

**HVAC Failure**:
1. Check thermostat and filter
2. Check circuit breaker
3. Call HVAC contractor
4. Use alternative heating/cooling if needed
5. Protect pipes in winter

**Gas Leak**:
1. Evacuate immediately
2. Call gas company/911 from outside
3. Don't use lights, phones, or appliances
4. Don't return until cleared by professionals
